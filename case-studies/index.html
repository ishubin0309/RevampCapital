The Romanian real estate market is attracting investors for several reasons. Firstly, the country's strong economic growth and political stability make it a favorable environment for investment. Secondly, the real estate prices in Romania are relatively low compared to other European countries, offering potential for significant returns on investment. GDP and employment growth indicate significant opportunity

From 2010 to 2019, Romania's GDP growth rates fluctuated, with some years witnessing robust expansion while others saw more modest growth. On average, Romania's GDP growth during this period was higher than the European Union average.

Romania's unemployment rate continued to show a downward trend. While the COVID-19 pandemic had a temporary impact, causing a rise in unemployment, the government implemented various support measures to mitigate the effects. The rate has been gradually declining as the country implemented reforms and worked to create a more favorable environment for job creation.
